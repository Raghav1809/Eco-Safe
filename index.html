The Problem

The Hidden Toxicity

Every year, 50+ million tonnes of e-waste are generated. Only 20% is recycled formally. The rest? It's handled in backyards, poisoning the soil we walk on and the air we breathe.

80%
Handled Informally

Toxic Heavy Metals

Lead, Mercury, and Cadmium accumulate in the body, causing irreversible damage.

Health Impact

Chronic exposure leads to kidney failure and bone density loss.

Airborne Dioxins

Burning plastic coatings releases cancer-causing chemicals into the local atmosphere.

Health Impact

Inhalation causes severe respiratory distress and lung cancer risk.

Water Leaching

Acids used to extract gold seep into groundwater, destroying local water supplies.

Health Impact

Contaminated water leads to gastrointestinal diseases and toxicity.

Genetic Risks

Exposure affects reproductive health and causes birth defects in communities.

Health Impact

Causes permanent DNA damage and severe neurodevelopmental issues.

Journey of Toxicity

How informal handling leads to irreversible health disasters

Informal Collection

Scrap dealers gather end-of-life electronics without protective gear, exposing themselves to initial lead and mercury dust.

Hazardous Processing

Burning wires for copper and acid-bathing motherboards for gold recovery releases highly carcinogenic dioxins into the air.

Chronic Illness

Long-term exposure leads to respiratory failure, neurological degeneration, and environmental contamination of the local food chain.
